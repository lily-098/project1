Electrical engineering

Electrical engineering is the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. In simple terms, it is about understanding how electricity works in order to use the knowledge for engineering applications. Electrical engineers defined the 20th century with technologies that transformed society. They gave us universal electric power, telecommunications, radio, and television. Today, we have access to electronic surveillance, medical imaging, computers, and the internet.

Electrical engineers work on a wide range of tasks including designing circuits for smartphones and computers, developing power systems for buildings and cities, connecting society with wireless communication networks, and maintaining modern civilization.

Circuit design is a starting point of electrical engineering. Circuits are like highways that allow electricity to flow through electronic devices. Electrical engineers design circuits that amplify signals, convert power from one form to another, or control the operation of a device. Power systems are another important topic that covers generation, transmission, and the distribution of electricity.
